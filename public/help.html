Make sure you called 911

The purpose of the app to reach nearby volunteers that can arrive and help before paramedics does. It is designed to assist, not replace 911 emergency call.

Try to keep them awake

If they’re conscious, ask questions to assess their level of alertness and to calmly keep them engaged, if possible. Try to get a response from them if they are not conscious.

Put them in recovery position

Turn them in the recovery position — on their side with the bottom arm under the head and top leg crossed over the body (see image below). This is to avoid aspiration if he or she vomits.

Obtain information

Obtain as much information as possible, including the dose and the last time the person took the drug.

Provide rescue breathing if necessary.

If you are medically qualified to do so, provide rescue breathing or CPR if necessary.

Administe Naloxone if trained

Note that naloxone is only effective in the case of an opioid overdose,but it is not known to cause any harm in the case of a non-opioid overdose. See directions below for how to properly administer naloxone.
